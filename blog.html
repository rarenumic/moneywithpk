What is Asset Allocation?

Asset allocation is the process of dividing investments among different asset categories, such as stocks, bonds, and cash, to optimize risk and return.

How Institutions Manage Risk Differently

Institutions often use diversification, hedging strategies, and rigorous analytics to manage portfolio risks more systematically than individual investors.

Modern Portfolio Theory Explained

Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) helps investors construct portfolios that maximize expected return based on a given level of market risk.

The Role of Behavioral Finance

Behavioral finance studies how psychological factors influence investor behavior and market outcomes, often leading to irrational financial decisions.
